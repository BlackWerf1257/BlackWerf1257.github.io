## Dynamic Programming

### Definition

- A technique for solving recursive problems by minimizing the number of recursive calls

### Examples of Use

- **A good example of a recursive approach**

  - Sorting algorithms (Quick sort, Merge sort, etc.)

  - Calculating factorials

  - DFS graph

- **A bad example of a recursive method**
  - Calculating Fibonacci numbers
 
 ​  The number of recursive calls increases significantly as the term number increases

### Eligibility Requirements

- **Optimal Substructure**
  - The optimal solution to a larger problem must include the optimal solutions to smaller problems
- **Redundancy in Recursive Calls**
  - When solved using a recursive approach, there is significant redundancy in the recursive calls for the same problem
